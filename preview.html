Advocacy

Children should not be punished before they are understood.

Too many children are pushed through schools and juvenile systems that punish behavior without asking why that behavior is happening. What people call defiance, disruption, anger, or shutting down may actually be stress, trauma, fear, unmet needs, or neurodivergence. When adults only react to behavior, children are denied the deeper support they truly need.

The Problem

The school-to-prison pipeline grows when punishment replaces understanding.

Systems too often respond to pain, dysregulation, and unmet needs with exclusion, discipline, and control instead of support.

The Cost

When adults do not ask why, children can be pushed into systems that harm them.

P2P advocates for looking deeper, supporting the brain, and interrupting pathways that punish children before understanding them.

Ask Why First

Behavior is often communication. We need to ask what a child is carrying, what the brain may be expressing, and what support is missing.

Support the Brain

Real help starts with understanding regulation, overwhelm, trauma, neurodivergence, and the deeper needs behind what is seen on the surface.

Fight for Better Systems

Caregivers, schools, and communities need practical tools that help them respond with purpose, not just punish what they do not understand.
